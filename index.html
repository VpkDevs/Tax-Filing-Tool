Understanding the Recovery Rebate Credit

What is the Recovery Rebate Credit?

The Recovery Rebate Credit is a tax credit related to the third Economic Impact Payment (stimulus payment) issued in 2021 as part of COVID-19 relief efforts. If you didn't receive the full amount you were entitled to, you may be able to claim the remaining amount as a credit on your tax return.

Third Stimulus Payment Details

The third stimulus payment was authorized by the American Rescue Plan Act of 2021 and provided:

  • Up to $1,400 for eligible individuals
  • Up to $2,800 for eligible married couples filing jointly
  • Up to $1,400 for each qualifying dependent

Important Deadlines

To claim the 2021 Recovery Rebate Credit, you must file a 2021 tax return, even if you're not typically required to file. The deadline to claim this credit is:

April 15, 2025 (three years from the original tax filing deadline)

Don't delay - file as soon as possible to receive your credit faster!

Important Note

The IRS is no longer issuing the third Economic Impact Payments directly. The only way to get this payment now is to claim the Recovery Rebate Credit on your 2021 tax return. Our automated filing tool will handle this entire process for you.

Eligibility Requirements

Basic Eligibility

To be eligible for the Recovery Rebate Credit, you must:

  • Be a U.S. citizen, permanent resident, or qualifying resident alien
  • Have a valid Social Security Number (SSN) issued before the due date of your 2021 tax return
  • Not be claimed as a dependent on someone else's tax return
  • Have not received the full amount of the third stimulus payment you were entitled to

Income Limits

The amount of the credit phases out based on your adjusted gross income (AGI):

  • Single filers: Begins phasing out at $75,000 AGI, fully phases out at $80,000
  • Head of household: Begins phasing out at $112,500 AGI, fully phases out at $120,000
  • Married filing jointly: Begins phasing out at $150,000 AGI, fully phases out at $160,000

Qualifying Dependents

Unlike previous stimulus payments, the third payment included eligible dependents of all ages, not just children under 17. Qualifying dependents include:

  • Children under 19 (or under 24 if a full-time student)
  • Adult dependents (including elderly parents and disabled adult children)
  • Must be claimed on your tax return
  • Must have a valid SSN or Adoption Taxpayer Identification Number (ATIN)
